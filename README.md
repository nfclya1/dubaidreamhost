## Shared vs VPS: Which One Do You Actually Need?

**Shared hosting** is the right call if:
- You're launching a new website or blog
- You expect moderate traffic (not tens of thousands of daily visitors)
- You want the simplest possible setup with cPanel and Softaculous
- Budget is a factor

**VPS hosting** is worth considering if:
- You're running an e-commerce store or SaaS application
- You expect high or spiky traffic
- You need persistent processes or custom server configurations
- You're an agency hosting multiple client sites and need isolated environments

The main difference: on shared hosting, your resources (CPU, RAM) are shared with other sites on the same server. On a VPS, your RAM is protected — other users can't eat into your allocation. LiteSpeed and Memcached help shared hosting punch above its weight, but if you're growing fast, VPS is the cleaner long-term call.
